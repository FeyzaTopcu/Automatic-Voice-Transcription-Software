"Fruit. Botanically, a fruit is a mature ovary of a flower. 

It contains the seed or seeds. it may or may not be edible, juicy, fleshy, or quite dry. 

Fruits come in many forms; some of the most familiar are achenes, berries, capsules, nuts, and pods. 

Some plant parts commonly called fruits, such a s blackberries, raspberries, strawberries, and pineapples, are really conglomerates of numerous little fruits each developed from a separate ovary. 

In this Encyclopedia the term fruit is used for these as well as for what are more correctly single fruits."
